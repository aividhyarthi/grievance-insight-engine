I've been thinking about starting a blog for years. I finally did it in 2026, and here's why.

## Learning in Public

There's a concept called "learning in public" that really resonated with me. The idea is simple: instead of keeping notes to yourself, share what you learn openly. Write blog posts, create tutorials, build in the open.

Why? Because:

- **Teaching solidifies understanding** — If you can explain it, you truly understand it
- **You help others** — Someone is always a step behind you and can benefit from your experience
- **It builds your portfolio** — Your blog becomes a living portfolio of your knowledge
- **It creates connections** — People find you through your writing and reach out

## Why Now?

A few things aligned:

1. I've been working on interesting AI projects and wanted to document them
2. The tools for blogging have gotten incredibly good (I built this site with Astro!)
3. I realized my "I'll start when I'm ready" was just procrastination

## My Plan

I'm going to write about:

- **AI and Machine Learning** — Practical applications, not just theory
- **Cloud Computing** — BigQuery, GCP, and scaling data pipelines
- **Building things** — Project walkthroughs and architecture decisions
- **Thoughts** — Broader ideas about tech, learning, and growth

## The Setup

This blog is built with Astro, deployed on Railway, and the code lives on GitHub. Simple, fast, and easy to maintain. I write posts in Markdown, push to GitHub, and Railway auto-deploys. No complex CMS needed.

If you've been thinking about starting a blog — just do it. The best time was yesterday. The second best time is now.
