If you have researched HRT even a little, you have likely come across the term "bioidentical hormones," often marketed as a safer, more natural alternative to standard treatment. The term causes genuine confusion, partly because it is used in two quite different ways, one referring to regulated, evidence-based medicine, and one referring to unregulated, custom-compounded products with much weaker evidence behind them. Understanding the distinction matters for making an informed decision.

## What "Bioidentical" Actually Means

Bioidentical simply means a hormone that is chemically identical, molecule for molecule, to the hormones your own body naturally produces, as opposed to hormones with a different chemical structure. This is a legitimate, meaningful scientific distinction, not marketing spin on its own.

Here is the important part many women do not realise: **many standard, regulated HRT products prescribed by gynaecologists already are bioidentical.** Body-identical oestradiol (in patches, gels, and tablets) and micronised progesterone are both bioidentical hormones, manufactured to pharmaceutical standards, tested for quality and dosing, and backed by the same evidence base discussed in our guide to whether HRT is safe. So if your doctor prescribes standard oestradiol and micronised progesterone, you are, in a real sense, already on bioidentical hormones.

## The Confusion: Compounded "Bioidentical" Hormones

Separately, there is a wellness industry offering **custom-compounded bioidentical hormones**, often mixed by compounding pharmacies into individualised creams, troches, or pellets, sometimes based on saliva hormone testing, and frequently marketed as more "natural," personalised, or safer than standard pharmaceutical HRT.

This is where real caution is warranted. These compounded products are generally **not regulated or tested with the same rigour** as standard pharmaceutical HRT. Their dosing can be inconsistent from batch to batch, they lack the large-scale safety and effectiveness studies that standard HRT has undergone, and claims that they are safer or more effective than regulated HRT are not supported by good evidence. Major medical and gynaecological bodies internationally have raised concerns about the lack of oversight and evidence for compounded bioidentical hormone products specifically.

Saliva hormone testing, often used to justify these customised prescriptions, is also not considered a reliable way to guide hormone dosing, because hormone levels naturally fluctuate throughout the day and across the cycle, making a single saliva sample an unreliable basis for treatment decisions.

## So What Should You Actually Ask For?

If the appeal of "bioidentical" is having a hormone that matches what your body naturally makes, you can have that through standard, regulated medicine. Ask your gynaecologist specifically about **body-identical oestradiol and micronised progesterone**, the standard, well-studied, pharmaceutically regulated bioidentical options widely available and prescribed. These give you the "bioidentical" quality many women are seeking, with the safety net of regulated manufacturing, consistent dosing, and a genuine evidence base behind them.

## Why the Marketing Persists

It is worth understanding, gently, why compounded bioidentical products are marketed so persuasively. Words like "natural," "customised," and "bioidentical" understandably appeal to women who have often felt dismissed or unheard by conventional medicine, and who are seeking something that feels more personal and less like a one-size-fits-all prescription. That desire is completely valid. The issue is not the wish for personalised, natural-feeling care, it is that compounded products marketed this way often do not deliver the safety and reliability their marketing implies, while standard regulated HRT can genuinely be tailored to you, in dose, form, and combination, by a knowledgeable doctor.

## What to Ask Your Doctor

- Is the oestrogen and progesterone you are recommending for me bioidentical, in the standard, regulated sense?
- Can standard HRT be personalised to my specific symptoms and needs?
- What is your view on compounded bioidentical products, and why?

## When to See a Doctor

**Routine appointment** to discuss standard, regulated HRT options, including bioidentical oestradiol and micronised progesterone, and to have your specific dose and delivery method tailored to you within that regulated framework.

**Before starting** any compounded hormone product from outside a standard medical prescription, discuss it with your gynaecologist first, so you understand what evidence does and does not exist for it, and whether a regulated alternative could meet the same need more safely.

"Bioidentical" is a real and meaningful term, but the safest, best-evidenced way to access bioidentical hormones is through standard, regulated medicine and a knowledgeable doctor, not through unregulated, custom-compounded products marketed as a more natural alternative. You do not have to choose between "natural" and "evidence-based." With standard HRT, in most cases, you can have both.
